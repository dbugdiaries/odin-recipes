Paneer Tikka

Description

Paneer Tikka is a popular Indian appetizer featuring cubes of paneer (Indian cottage cheese) marinated in a spiced yogurt blend and grilled until charred and smoky. It’s perfect for parties or as a starter to an Indian meal.

The yogurt marinade infuses the paneer with vibrant flavors of cumin, coriander, and garam masala, while the char from the grill adds an irresistible depth.

Ingredients

  • 250 g paneer, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1 bell pepper, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 onion, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • ¾ cup plain yogurt
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp oil (plus extra for brushing)
  • Salt, to taste
  • Fresh coriander, chopped (for garnish)

Steps

  1. In a bowl, whisk together yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric, garam masala, cumin, coriander, lemon juice, oil, and salt to make a smooth marinade.
  2. Add paneer cubes, bell pepper, and onion to the marinade. Gently toss to coat everything evenly. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Preheat your grill or grill pan to medium-high heat. Brush with a little oil to prevent sticking.
  4. Thread marinated paneer, pepper, and onion alternately onto skewers.
  5. Place skewers on the grill. Cook for 3-4 minutes per side, turning occasionally, until charred spots appear and vegetables are tender-crisp.
  6. Remove from heat, transfer to a serving platter, and sprinkle with chopped coriander.
  7. Serve hot with mint-coriander chutney and lemon wedges.
